Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Find quick answers about using CampusConnect.

How do I register for any event?

+
Open the event card on the dashboard, click Fill Form, complete the form, then click Confirm Registration to finish registering for the event.

How can I add events to my wishlist?

+
Click the heart (❤️) icon on any event card. View and manage saved events from the Wishlist section in your dashboard.

Why can’t I see some events?

+
Only approved events are visible to students. Events with a status of pending or denied will not appear on the public list until approved by the faculty coordinator.

I forgot my account details. What should I do?

+
Contact your coordinator or admin with the email address you used to register. They can help recover or reset your account information.

How do I change my password?

+
Use the account settings page (My Account) to change your password if your account supports password changes. If not available, contact support or your administrator to reset it.

What is the difference between Student Number and Faculty ID?

+
Students have a Student Number and faculty users have a Faculty ID. Your account page will display the appropriate identifier depending on your role.

How do I create or manage a club?

+
Faculty coordinators can create clubs from the club management area of the dashboard. Provide a name and description; the club will be available after creation.

How long does event approval take?

+
Approval time depends on the faculty coordinator's review. Typically events are reviewed within a few business days; you'll receive a notification after approval or denial.

Who do I contact for technical issues?

+
For site issues or bugs, contact the admin team at admin@campusconnect.example (replace with your institution's support email) and include details about the problem and screenshots if possible.

How is my data handled?

+
CampusConnect stores only the necessary account and event registration data. Please refer to your institution's privacy policy for details on retention and sharing.
